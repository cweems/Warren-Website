It's time to act.





Interest rates for students will jump from 3.4 to 6.8 percent.
That's 9x what big banks pay to loan money from the government.

Student debt is huge. Why make it bigger?


Student debt in the United States is nearly $1 trillion.
That's more than all U.S. credit card debt combined.


Why is that a big deal?



The burden of loans keeps young Americans from buying homes, saving for retirement, and engaging in consumption that will jump-start our economy.

Interest rates for students are already too high.

While students struggle to repay their debt,
the government is making money on its student loan programs.

Estimated government profits on student loans in 2013:

$34 billion.

That's enough to pay for every college student's textbooks for a year.







"The Federal Government is going to charge students interest rates nine times higher than the rates they charge the biggest banks.
The same banks that destroyed millions of jobs and nearly broke the economy. That isn't right."

Sen. Elizabeth Warren

The Plan:

Set interest rates for government loans to students at the same level as government loans to banks.

Fund the Department of Education's Stafford Loans program the same way that we fund government loans to big banks: The Federal Reserve.
